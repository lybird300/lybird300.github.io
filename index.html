A Social Scientist's Journey to Computational Genetics

“It is good to have an end to journey toward; but it is the journey that matters, in the end.” ― Ernest Hemingway

April 1st, 2015, the April Fool’s Day is the first day that I started as a post-doc fellow in the Department of Genetics at the School of Medicine in University of North Carilina at Chapel Hill. The person bold enough to hire me for this job, Dr. Kirk Wilhelmsen, stopped by after lunch. “Whatever you don’t know, I will teach you,” he said when he left. This world-known geneticist and neurologist offered to give me a one-hour exclusive lecture every Friday. “Geez,” I said to myself, “you’d better not screw up.”
